Paper Grading, chooses the first paper you've uploaded and shows for you to use as an answer key. Let's look at how to upload scanned papers before we talk about it.

First, navigate to Grade screen via " Grade" button.

If you've associated some questions with learning objectives while creating your exam paper, please enter "Exam Code" that you can see on the left side of your paper in "Exam Code" field. This way Paper Grading will understand which exam it is about to grade and analyze learning objective performance of students automatically.

Then choose exam language and subject. Also choose how many sides your exam has, for example if your exam has a rear page, you need to choose two. Finally, click "Upload exam papers" and select all the scanned exam papers which are in correct order. In order to select everything in a folder, you can use CTRL + A shortcut on Windows, Command + A on Mac.

After uploading the papers, Paper Grading will redirect you to answer key screen.

It's really easy to create an answer key. You can zoom in and out on the paper on this screen which is designed both for desktop and mobile devices.

Click every green button you see on the screen and enter the correct answer(s) and question points.

For multiple choice questions, write only the correct option, for example "A" or "E".

For true false questions, write "True" or "False" .

For matching questions, write only the correct letter. For example, "H" or "K".

For gap filling questions, write all the possible correct answers students might give seperating them with a comma. For example, "London, Capital City, Capital of England".

After you've filled every question, use "Save and Continue" button under the exam paper image.

You can use buttons on the bottom right to navigate through the exam paper sides, for example "Paper 1" or "Paper 2". Don't forget that these papers are first student's papers.

If you don't enter answers for every question, "Save and Continue" button won't work.

That's all you need to do. You can take a look at "Getting The Reports" guide to see how to generate automatic reports both for your class and a specific student.
