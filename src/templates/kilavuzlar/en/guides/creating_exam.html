Creating an exam that Paper Grading can grade without worring about exam layout is very easy. You can create an instantly downloadable exam paper as PDF on Exam Creator, just click on the button on the left side of navigation bar " Create"

When you click Create button, you'll see "Create An Exam" button under the title "Your Exams". Click that button and proceed.

After clicking this button, you can specify the characteristics of the exam and enter the questions on the left side of the screen that will appear.. 

Before creating the exam, please fill required fields on the main form. After specifying the lesson, please specify at least one topic if required. If you can't find a topic that is needed for your exam, get in touch with us.

Additionally, you can change the layout of the exam or you can make this exam visible to everybody.  


When you make your exam visible, that exam will be shown in Exam Archive. This way other teachers can download, make a copy of it for themselves, or like your exam. When your exam is liked, you'll get Paper Grading points.

After you finish filling required fields, click "+ Add A Question" button. The options that will pop up on this button, represents the question types that Paper Grading can grade.

Reminder: "Update" button that you see on right top of your corner is your friend. You should use that button after every change you make.


Let's look at those question types in detail.

Adding Multiple Choice Question

After entering the required question statement to add a multiple choice question, enter at least four options. Paper Grading will place the options in the most efficient way.

You can change the style of the question statement by making it bold, italic or underlined. You can also add math or chemistry formulas to the question statement by clicking the rightmost button. For this, you can draw a formula using your mouse.

Like the question statements, you can change the style of the options. To do this, select a text of the option with left click, and the style bar will open for the text you selected.

You can also use formulas or images as multiple choice question options.

For this click "Other options".

When you click "Upload Images" option, you can use at least four images as options.

Warning: Every image you upload should have an unique file name. If you upload another file with the same name in another question, the images you originally uploaded may change. Therefore, it is recommended to give numbers to the images. For example: Question1-Image1.jpg, Question1-Image2.jpg, Question5-Image1.jpg , Question6-Image1.jpg.

After uploading images, you can specify how the options will be placed under "Options Layout" setting.

"Add Formula" option lets you draw a maths or chemistry formula with your mouse so that you can add it as an option.

Adding Matching Question

Let's look at how we can add matching questions. After writing a question statement, you can add a set of matching items clicking "+" button which is on the right side of the default set.

Reminder: You can't add more than 11 sets. For now, Paper Grading can identify A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, K letters.

After you've done, click Update button which is on the right top of your screen to transfer that question to the exam paper.

"Put the questions one under the other" setting lets you place every matching set one under the other.

Adding True False Question

Adding true false questions is pretty straightforward. After choosing true false question, write the question statement in the first field, after that add a set of true-false question clicking "+" button which is next to the default set. You can add as many as true-false sets as you want.

Adding Gap Filling Question

When you add gap filling question, you will see three fields. As we already know, the first field is for question statement.

Second field is for helper words for students to get tips from which is totally optional. For example you can write:

article - story - interview - anectode

as helper words for students to choose from.

For gaps that should be filled by students, you need to use underscores ( ______ ) in the third field.

Reminder: Please use at least three underscores. Nothing except for underscores will be identified as a gap question in Paper Grading. For example:

CORRECT: Capital city of England is _________.

This is a correct way of adding a gap filling question but for instance if you put dots instead of underscores, Paper Grading will not understand that this is a gap filling question:

WRONG: Capital city of England is ..............

Adding An Image

You can also add images to your exam papers. Don't forget this rule as we've mentioned before: every image you upload should have an unique name, or else your exam paper will go crazy.


Adding an image is very easy as well. Via the upload button which pops up when you click add image button, choose the image from your computer and hit Update button to transfer that onto your exam paper. You can also change its size.

Reminder: If you're creating a question with an image associated with it, don't forget to add the image before the question.

Changing The Order

If you've entered all the questions, congratulations! We hope you've liked it.

Let's look at the exam paper again, if you want to move some questions around, you can do that by clicking the question card (white area around the question) and click the "Settings" symbol at the right side of the question card. You'll see three options there, change the order of the question clicking "↑" or "↓" buttons, or you can delete the question with "X" button.

Adding A Learning Objective

If you associate a question with a learning objective, you can get learning objectives report after the exam so easily. It's very easy to associate an objective with a question. After adding all the questions, click on ".... associated learning objectives" statement on the right top corner of the question card.

Reminder: If there's no learning objective belonging to the subject area that you've chosen in our database, you can't add a learning objective to that exam paper. If you want us to add a learning objective to a specific subject, kindly get in touch with us.

You can associate maximum three learning objectives with multiple choice questions, maximum one for each set of true-false and matching questions and maximum one for each gap of gap-filling questions.

Using Exam Archive

You can navigate to Exam Archive via the button on the right top of screen on Your Exams page.

You can filter the exams using topic and grade filters on the right side.  

You can like interesting exams as a "thank you", or you can make a copy for yourself and edit that exam or easily download it.

After you copy the exam successfully Paper Grading will redirect you to edit page. You can remove or add questions on that exam paper as you wish and print it to distribute.
